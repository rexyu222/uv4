Washington —  Opening remarks: Such an alien group lives in the northeast region of China. Many of them can speak fluent Chinese, and some have lived in China for more than ten or twenty years. Many women intermarry with locals and have children. However, they do not have the legal status of China. According to Chinese law, they are “illegal immigrants” and they must be arrested and sent back to their motherland, North Korea, separated from China by the risk of their lives. This foreign group is the Koreans known as the North Korean defectors. What kind of people are they? Why take such a big risk to escape from the homeland? After China sends them back to North Korea, what is waiting for them?

Zhao Zhenhui is a North Korean defector. She and her family fled to China several times in the past 10 years, and was sent back to North Korea several times before finally arriving in the United States.

This river is called Tumen River. The river is not wide, and the water is not deep during non-flood seasons. It is the boundary river between China and North Korea. The north bank of the river is Yanbian Korean Autonomous Prefecture in Jilin Province, China, and the south bank is North Hamgyong Province of North Korea. Zhao Zhenhui was born there in 1987.

*Famine: Go to China with the last effort*

Zhao Zhenhui: Almost when I was about eight years old, North Korea started to have no food and started to be hungry. (Previously) The country distributes food every month, but it stopped almost six months after Kim Il Sung died. Just call during the New Year or Kim Il Sung or Kim Jong Il's birthday, you can watch TV. That TV is not its own. There is a TV and radio in the kind of place in the village where you can listen.

Li Su: What can you see and hear?

Zhao Zhenhui: It's all about how Kim Il Sung established this country, how Kim Jong Il learned, and how do we keep up with him. Singing and dancing are for Kim Jong Il, and we will serve him even if we die. When I first started kindergarten, my own thoughts were: Grandpa Kim Il Sung, we said at that time that Kim Il Sung was a grandpa, and Kim Jong Il was a dad, a great general. At that time, I thought that when I grew up, my dream was to dance or sing. After I learnt well, I would sing in front of them to make them happy and think about these things.

When I started to feel hungry, I never thought about it (so much). I might be too young, I never thought why I was so hungry. When they went to school, they said that the great Kim Jong Il did these things for the country, those things. They said a lot of such things. Then say, do you know why we are so hungry? We said, don't know. In other words, they have big noses and blue eyes. People like them who are like demons threaten us North Korea. Foreign countries give us food, but this country does not allow it, so we North Korea are so bitter. But we must guard North Korea, because they like that there is a lot of gold in North Korea. In order to grab this, they want to destroy our country. So if we are hungry, or if we are going to starve to death, we must also guard this country. That was the first time I heard, oh, it was the Yankees, these people, these bastards treated us like this, so we are so bitter.

Voice-over: In the early 1990s, North Korea began to experience severe food shortages. In 1991, the Soviet Union disintegrated, and North Korea lost its largest donor. Mistakes in agricultural policies, external economic blockades, and years of bad weather have brought North Korea's economy on the verge of collapse, and there have been years of continuous famine. Because of the news of the North Korean government’s blockade, people cannot know the exact number of deaths from the famine. However, sporadic information obtained from some research institutions and the North Korean population who fled abroad, the outside world can still glimpse the severity of the famine.

Huang Changye, the former No. 2 figure in North Korea, defected to South Korea in 1997. In his memoir, "I understand the true meaning of history," he quoted data from the Central Organization Department of the Workers' Party of Korea, saying: "In 1995, 500,000 people were starved to death, including 50,000 party members, and this year (1996) was hungry. 1 million people died." A

1998 field survey report of the International Relations Committee of the US House of Representatives said that from 1995 to 1998, about 900,000 to 2.4 million people in North Korea died of food shortages, which may be as high as ten One in one.

In 2007, the South Korean non-governmental organization "North Korean Association of North Korean Fugitives" said that during the 60 years of the Kim family's administration, at least 3.5 million people died from hunger and related diseases.

Zhao Zhenhui: Then my dad thought, I heard that there is food in China, can we go there? Sometimes my parents will not come back for a week or two. As soon as they came back, they carried rice with a little oil or something. It seems that I went there five times. When I came back, I told us some Chinese stories. I started thinking, why is North Korea so hard? China is next to a river. Why does China have so much food to eat? I thought about this.

Voiceover: Although it is just across the river, crossing the Tumen River into China is a journey of desperation. The North Korean government regards North Korea as a crime of treason, and North Korean border guards often shoot people who try to cross the river to escape to the other side; on the North Korean border, the bodies of North Korean defectors are not uncommon. In the harsh political environment of North Korea, anyone who leaves the country without permission, if found, must be sent to prison or a labor camp as a political prisoner.

Even so, people continue to flee to China across the border. The famous North Korean human rights expert David Hawke said in his book "The Hidden Gulag" in 2003: "It is estimated that as many as 200,000 to 300,000 people crossed the border and fled into China in the mid-to-late 1990s. A

report from the American Christian Science Monitor on May 11, 2012 said that Kim Sang-hoon, director of the North Korean Human Rights Data Center in South Korea, estimated that there are currently an estimated 100,000 to 200,000 North Koreans living in China. Their accurate figures are almost impossible to count, because they are engaged in inconspicuous work and are mixed with more than 2 million Chinese Koreans.

In North Korea, the government also encourages citizens to supervise and report against each other in order to prevent the North Koreans. Zhao Zhenhui’s parents were sentenced to jail for being in China.

Zhao Zhenhui: Someone was arrested first. He is also a public security bureau in the Ministry of National Security of North Korea. However, it is a very serious place, and most people can't get in there. After going there, the officials of the government told him that if you confess to other crimes or other people did bad things, you can alleviate your crimes. So they knew that my dad and my mom had been to China, so my dad and my mom were arrested.

Listening to my mother, they beat my dad with a stick and broke the stick. When my mother looked at my dad, he collapsed to the ground with a pool of blood beside him. My father does not admit his charges. The North Korean government said: You are a party member, but if you go to China, it is tantamount to betraying the country. Who did you meet after you went there, did you become a spy? My dad didn't admit it when he died. The last soldiers said that your wife is already pregnant. If you don’t admit it, the child in the belly may have to go to prison with you. Then they may die. Then my dad said, if I confess, can you let them go? Then my dad was painting and they let my mom go, so she went home.

Li Su: Did your father come back later?

Zhao Zhenhui: No.

Li Su: Never come back?

Zhao Zhenhui: Those criminals who stayed with my dad came to our house to tell us how my dad died. He said that he and my dad were sent by train to another prison, and they didn't give food. After ten days of staying there, he starved to death on the train.

Voice-over: Later, Zhao Zhenhui’s sister was sold to China by traffickers, and the youngest brother starved to death two months after birth. Later, Grandma also died.

Zhao Zhenhui: My mother didn't have the strength to cry, changed her clothes and helped her wash. There was no money at home and she could not buy her a coffin. After visiting the village, the villagers gave us some plastic cloth and the like, wrapped her with the quilt of her grandmother, and dragged the grandmother to the mountain with a cow and buried it. When I was going to bury, I asked them why they buried grandma so far away? The people in the village said that when your grandma was alive, she told us that after I died, I wanted to see my little children walk out of the mountain alive. After I returned home, I told my mother that I was going to die here anyway. Give it a try and try it with my last effort. Then I carried my younger brother, took my sister's hand, and helped my mother out of the mountain. Because this is grandma's last wish.

After we walked out like this, we put our younger brother in one's home because I was so tired that I couldn't carry him. So I put him there and told him that we will come back to find you in five days. But he grabbed my mother and my hands and said: "Mom, why do you have to take Miss Sister, why don't you take me?" I replied to him, "Mother and Sister go to carry some rice, and Sister Sister might have to carry Come with sugar and cookies, so the three of us have to go together." He said, "Yes." So we came to China.

But five days later, when going back, it rained too much and the river was too deep to pass. Second, Kim Jong Il did the presidential vote (election) at the time, every four years. He said that the Korean people who did not participate in the voting ceremony would have to be shot, so we dare not go back. Two months later, we found someone, gave him a little money, and asked him to carry my brother over. But he came back empty-handed and told us that the life of the family that raised my brother was also very difficult. It's really impossible to get him out. It means that my mother gave birth to six, leaving me and my sister now. So sometimes, I really miss him. Although I have come to the United States now, but when I see a child, I think of my younger brother. When I see an older person, I think of my dad, and when I see an old man, I think of my grandma, so I think that I am not mentally free.

*In China: Dignity is not an invisible person*

Li Su: Where did you first go to China, and where did you settle down?

Zhao Zhenhui: When we first crossed the river, I forgot the name of the village. That belongs to Helong County, Jilin Province and Helong County. When we first crossed the river, we saw potatoes, corn, cucumbers and the like all over the ground. We feel very scary, how can nobody steal it? Because in North Korea, there must be a lot of people in this place to take care of these, but (here) no one takes care of them. We were so hungry that we picked corn, and peeled and ate it raw. We also picked cucumbers and the like, and ate while walking.

After walking for a while, we went to a village where a grandma and grandpa lived. This place is where my parents lived when they came here before. As soon as I walked in the door, I saw that there was a lot of rice left over on the kang. I asked them if they could eat this? They said yes. My sister and I ran to eat. After more than an hour, they let us eat. After eating, I couldn't keep it up. I took medicine while diarrhea. Anyway, when I came here for a month, I knew to eat every day and thought about eating all day.

The next morning, when I came out together, I was ignorant. Because the grandmother sprinkled some rice, a little broken, bad rice scattered on the ground. Chickens, dogs, pigs, all ran over to eat the rice. I see that the potato next to it is outside, and the pig goes over there to eat the potato. I can't say anything. Then I cried. Grandma and mother asked me why I cried, did it hurt somewhere? I said, no. When my grandmother died, didn't she want to eat potatoes, but the potatoes that all pigs can eat, why don't we have them at home? That was the biggest blow since I came to China. Later, the grandma helped my mother find someone, and it was my later father, Chinese, and Korean.

Li Su: Your mother married him?

Zhao Zhenhui: Right. Almost a year later, when playing with the children in the village. A boy accidentally fell and he scolded me. I don’t know what he’s talking about, but I look like he’s scolding me. Whatever he said, I followed what he said, and he said me: bastard. I followed and said: Wang Ba egg. At the end he cried in anger. After returning home, I asked my father: "What does this mean?" My father said: "This is a curse, why do you say this?" Later his mother ran over and said to me: "You child who rolls from North Korea, Why bully my child?" She scolded me like this. I thought my mother would be very angry and quarrel with her. But my mother didn't say a word, apologized to her, and said, "I'm sorry, I will talk to my children in the future," and then took my hand home. I was angry on the side: "His mother is angry with me for his children. Why can't you be angry for me?" Mom said to me: "Do you want to go home? Go back to North Korea?" I said: "No "My mother said: "If my mother is angry with them, they might want to sue us and let us go back to North Korea." My mother said: "If you can bear it, don't play with it." That's all. Then I started to be very careful and try not to play with the children.

Voice-over: Living so carefully is out of fear of China’s official policy of forced repatriation of North Korean defectors.

Chinese state media reported in March 2012: According to South Korean media, the defectors will be subjected to persecution and ill-treatment after being repatriated. (Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs spokesperson) Hong Lei said that these reports have a tendency to be emotional and politicized. The actual situation. According to China's control, some North Koreans who have entered the country illegally have been repeatedly repatriated many times, some as many as more than ten times. Their illegal entry was mainly for economic purposes and there was no sufficient basis to characterize them as refugees.

Hong Lei said: "I want to reiterate that China's judicial dignity deserves to be respected and safeguarded. In any country, illegal smuggling, organized smuggling and other criminal behaviors are not allowed."

In 2001, Zhao Zhenhui and his mother were arrested by the Chinese police for the first time Arrested and sent back to North Korea.

*North Korea: You are prisoners equal to dogs*

Zhao Zhenhui: I walked for about four hours and arrived at a prison. The Ministry of National Security and Protection is the highest place in North Korea (prison level). I stayed there for almost a month. At that time, the North Korean government had regulations that children could not be kept in prison, so we stayed there for two weeks and sent us to an orphanage. North Korea specially detained those who were homeless or had mothers, but after they were arrested Children who want to be separated from their mothers. Keep us there, let us study and review all day, and state our crimes. After talking, many children scolded us and said something about us. Anyway, don't treat us like people.

After staying there for over a month, my mother came to pick us up. My mother asked me, "Do you want to stay here?" I thought about it and said, "Can we go? If we can go, let's go." Because I stayed in jail for a month and then stayed with the children in the orphanage When I was thinking about China. I regret that at that time, why didn't I finish the thing? Then I thought, oh, that rice. I thought about a lot of things, all about food. At that time it was still small, I didn't think about anything else, I just wanted to go to China.

Ten days later, we started to cross the river. When crossing the river, my mother prepared the money. After she was caught there (China), she took Chinese money, about a thousand dollars, wrapped it up, almost covered such a large amount of money with her fingers, wrapped it very tightly, and then wrapped it with plastic cloth , Tied it again with a string, tied several layers, and then she swallowed. It means that after the money is pulled out, the parcel must be cut out before it can be used. But when you want to go to the toilet in prison, you can't cut it because there is a Camera on the toilet, and they are monitoring that.

Li Su: There is a camera monitoring.

Zhao Zhenhui: Yes, so it simply won't work. That's pretty good. Once a day, we took us to the toilet, and all the people had to go. It was outside, inside the field, and a lot of soldiers stood by to watch. They said: "Don't think you are female, shy or something, you are just prisoners, it's like a dog." They said so to us. At the beginning we were really shy and did not dare to go. But after a long time, I don't want to think about anything. Some people have to pick up the money after pulling it. If they were caught, they were beaten hard, and the money was taken away by them. That night, the meals will be better. Originally very thin porridge, but the quality will be a little better, put a little more corn, and then said: "Whose blessing is this."

Li Su: His money was confiscated?

Zhao: Right. Say blessings to anyone. They also said: "Try to eat as much as possible, and go to the toilet tomorrow morning." Then he went out. In order to save this money, my mother did not eat for two weeks. After it was finally released, I picked up the money, gave it to the soldier when I crossed the river, and then passed.

*Failed to break the embassy in South Korea* Broken

voiceover: When Zhao Zhenhui and his mother crossed the Tumen River for the second time, the Chinese public security department was stepping up investigations of North Korean defectors. There was a rush of noise in Yanbian. A report from China’s 21st Century Global Report in 2002 quoted locals as saying: "It is basically impossible for North Koreans to live in cities now. Because of strict inspections, once they are found, anyone who allows North Korean stowaways to work Both the shop owner and the person hosting them are fined." It is reported that in July of that year, the neighborhood committees of Tumen asked each family to sign an agreement: to ensure that no North Koreans were admitted; "Once found, the host fined 1,000 yuan, the reporter A reward of 500 yuan and a reward of 100 yuan for those who provide clues."

Li Su: So after you came out from there (stepfather), where did your whole family go?

Zhao Zhenhui: Hou went to Shenyang for the first time. One person introduced a job to my mother, and that was a Korean elder's home. At their home, we watched TV for the first time, Korean TV (program), before it was Chinese TV (program). After we watched Korean TV, we were ashamed, because we used to learn to eat rice in South Korea. There are many beggars and live very hard. Americans live in South Korea and bully Koreans, so South Korea is a very Poor place. But when we watched the movie, it was not the case at all.

The elder, the clothes he wore, what he said, the coffee he drank, we all felt weird. How do you drink bitter coffee? His friends, Koreans, always come over and wear suits when they come. They dressed like that when they went to church on Sunday. Once told us to go to church together. We have always studied the Bible, but we are all at home and dare not go to church, because once we go to church, we may be arrested. But those Koreans say that it is a Korean church, so you can rest assured. As soon as we went to see, the older women, dressed up, dressed like princesses, so Hou was embarrassed then, oops, this is a bit strange, and then began to ask him (Korea elder) Korean Some situations. That grandpa let us look at the computer and began to surf the Internet slowly. Then we thought, in such a good place, the language can be understood, because we can understand what they say. How about we go to this place?

Once, someone said that he could go to South Korea, and he wanted to help us. He said, "When you arrive in South Korea, give us money." They demand a lot of money. We asked: "We don't have that much money, how do you give it to you?" He said: "If North Koreans come to South Korea, they give a house, give money, give food, live. So, you can rest assured, just put Just give me a third of your money.” We said, “Yes.” Then follow him to Beijing, and then say that he will enter a place tomorrow morning. We went with 12 people, and one of the North Koreans said, "Okay, let's run in from here." Mom said, "Keep up with Mom." Then she ran in. It's about two meters high, the barbed wire. My mother jumped in at once. The remaining one jumped in, and later three women jumped in. I was a bit fat at that time, about 65 kg. I never climbed that kind of wall. As soon as I looked at my sister standing next to me so small, I thought, ah, maybe I can't do it anymore, let her go first. I put her on it and let her pass that (barbed wire). Halfway through her, a man in military uniform came to pick her up. I was ashamed at first glance, what's going on? Didn’t you say you wouldn’t be caught? At first glance, there were many people nearby, almost 20 people, chasing in from both sides. When I looked back, three people came to catch me. I beat them with a bag, and when I was about to run, someone fell on my head and I fell. It really doesn't hurt, the legs don't work well, the neck hurts and I can't lift my head.

Li Su: Do you know where you are going to escape?

Zhao Zhenhui: They said it was an embassy.

Li Su: whose embassy?

Zhao Zhenhui: I didn't say which country it belongs to.

Li Su: Both you and your sister were arrested. Did your mother go in?

Zhao Zhenhui: Go in.

Li Su: Not caught?

Zhao Zhenhui: I hadn't been arrested at that time, but after about 30 minutes after I got on the bus, my mother also came. My mother was also arrested.

Voice-over: Due to China’s compulsory repatriation policy, North Korean defectors hope to travel to a third country through China. In recent years, China has repeatedly had incidents of North Korean defectors breaking into foreign institutions in China. The video circulating on the Internet is a picture of a family of five in North Korea trying to enter the Japanese consulate in Shenyang to seek asylum in May 2002. At that time, a foreign journalist happened to film the scene opposite. The little girl in North Korea who was overwhelmed by the sight in front of her was called Kim Han-mei, who was only two years old. She looked at the helpless, crying face of her mother and Chinese security guards when she was pulling, and she didn't know how many people had moved. Jin Hanmei and his family finally arrived in South Korea, but Zhao Zhenhui's family was not so lucky that time.

Li Su: Where have the three of you been taken?

Zhao Zhenhui: Detained in a prison of a public security bureau in Beijing.

Li Su: How long did it go?

Zhao Zhenhui: Ten days.

Li Su: Is it released or sent back to North Korea?

Zhao Zhenhui: Returned to North Korea. When we were walking on the train, they said that we should give North Korea our information and the time we were caught. So I thought about it for a while and started to say, "If you send us back to North Korea like this, our family will die. I cried and told them how my brother died and how my father died. So we want to live, can you help us." There is a woman there who looks good, she is willing to help me. There are two other men, the two who beat me three days and three nights. They said, "I didn't speak for three days and three nights, now I speak." They didn't want to help me. The woman quarreled with them and said, "She didn't speak for the sake of life, but for nothing else." She slammed against these two men and helped me, so she tore up all of our information. , Opened the window and flew away.

After being sent back like this, every month they beat my mother and let my mother tell the truth. But my mother said: "I didn't know that was the embassy at that time. Someone told me that there was my eldest daughter in there, and I wanted to see my daughter, so I went in. But I wasn't aware of this until I was arrested. "She didn't admit it before she was released, and we ran away again."

*Females from North China: victims of human trafficking*

Li Su: Once again escaping and crossing the river?

Zhao Zhenhui: Right. The same way.

Li Su: After crossing the river, where did you go?

Zhao Zhenhui: The three of us cannot come together and let my mother and my sister cross the river first. After the two of them arrived in China, they called me. Sneaking into a person's house, people made a rumor: "It's safe, you will come again in a few days." But my mother was caught halfway. I heard that after she was arrested, I would first come to China by myself and find some money to return to North Korea, and wanted to redeem my mother.

Li Su: Was she sent back again?

Zhao Zhenhui: Right. After I came to China, I found my sister. I started to make money and work. When they were working part-time, they were accidentally recognized as North Koreans, so they didn't give them money, and I went to another place. When I was working there, I didn't say my identity. When I thought they would know me soon, I would run again. Sometimes when working, people from the Public Security Bureau check the household registration and those from other places. When I checked, I ran away from the back door. Later, when I called the boss, I said that I am in such an identity. Can my salary be paid to me? Give me some kindhearted people. Some say that you are going to run yourself. If you tell us in advance, we will help you. I just think the money will be pulled down. After a few months, I saw my mother. After my mother was released, someone wanted to abduct my mother.

Voice-over: Unfamiliar environments and language barriers make female defectors easily victims of human trafficking. It is not uncommon to spend a few thousand yuan to buy a North Korean bride. In addition, South Korea’s “North Korean Human Rights Data Center” estimates that about 20,000 to 30,000 North Korean women are forced into prostitution or sex slaves in China.

Zhao Zhenhui: I have almost the same phone numbers as the traffickers of Long and Yanji. I asked from door to door, leave a message, and tell them that if such a woman is found, I will give you 3000 yuan, give me this woman. These people called and told me that it seemed to be your mother, she came, somewhere in Shenyang. When I went, those people were about to sell my mother. I went to see her, and ran away from there. After running away, he went to a man's house, where he met a pastor. The pastor is now in Seattle, Seattle, USA. After seeing this person, he began to give us where we lived, and gave us food and clothing.

*Go through the desert and go to Mongolia to escape, and then become empty*

Voice-over: The pastor Zhao Zhenhui and his mother called Philip Barker. Buck was born in North Korea in 1941. During the Korean War in the 1950s, he separated from his family and fled to South Korea, and later immigrated to the United States. Since 1994, he has been preaching in China. By raising funds in the United States, Buck established a North Korean refugee asylum network in China. The United States' "Times Weekly" website once compared it to the "underground railway" system that helped black slaves escape to freedom during the American Civil War in the 19th century. Buck and Zhao Zhenhui's mother and daughter met in 2005. At that time, the defectors began to open up new escape routes. They usually choose two routes: one is to travel more than half of China, pass through Laos, enter Thailand, surrender to the authorities there, and be sent to South Korea. The other one was planned by Pastor Buck at the time, from northeast China to Inner Mongolia, through the Gobi Desert and into Mongolia. Mongolia recognizes the refugee status of the defectors. From there they can travel freely to third countries.

Zhao Zhenhui: After we met the pastor, we spent more than six months with him. Then the priest began to prepare money, and one of the people who led the way was asking for too high a price of 7,000 yuan. This pastor has more than 30 North Koreans who need help, and they all want to go to South Korea. He didn't have so much money, he sighed and said to me: "How can I help them?" I said: "So, I can speak Chinese, find two smart people to give them money first, let them go. They are half Which station, which restaurant, etc. stopped on the road, give me those names, and give me the address of the area. I can give it a try."

Li Su: At that time, you haven't ran out, how can you think of helping others run out together? What?

Zhao Zhenhui: Because when I was young, the church people have helped me since I was 13 years old. I knew this pastor. When I was 14 years old, in Helong County, there was a religious person who helped us. He fed us for about a year. He gave us money, food and housing. I was very young at the time and thought he was great. I help others by repaying him. So I started to want to help these 30 people go by, and finally our family went by.

Li Su: Did the escape succeed this time?

Zhao Zhenhui: The person who went for the first time listened to my words. According to what I meant, I walked a few kilometers, turned around and walked a few kilometers over the wire (net), and then called me and said, "It's over." Let me go and let them go, but when these people went, they brought a five-year-old child and an old man. After a while, they felt tired, because of the desert, they walked very hard. It is very cold at night and very hot during the day. It was cold, and the wind was very strong that night, so they passed the wire (net) after a while. The soldiers in charge of the border saw someone coming, so they were caught.

Li Su: The place where they crossed the wire (net) is wrong.

Zhao Zhenhui: Yes, it takes more time to pass. They were about to pass in a while, so they were caught. After being arrested, the Chinese Public Security Bureau deceived them, saying that you only need to name the person behind you, we want to fine him, and then we can let you go. They believed this, and they all said it. One of these five people was cheating. He was instructed by the North Korean government to come to China and specifically catch these North Koreans who want to go to South Korea.

Li Su: It is equivalent to being a spy.

Zhao Zhenhui: Yes, he wanted to go to South Korea himself, so after he set off, he informed the public security officer of the latter batch, so the latter batch was already targeted by the Public Security Bureau. He thought that he would run to South Korea first, and the people in the back might be caught, but he accidentally was caught in the front, and the people in the back were also caught. So the American pastor, me and my mother were arrested in various regions.

The person who told us said that there were more than 50 North Koreans who had to pass by from here. Only a dozen people were arrested, and the rest ran away. They want to know their information from my mouth. I can’t coax, I can’t do it, I can’t do it for three days and three nights. I can’t hold it anymore. I passed out, my body was swollen and I couldn’t wear a coat.

Li Su: The Chinese police hit it?

Zhao Zhenhui: Yes, China is a soldier, and the frontier is a soldier.

Li Su: Ready to send you back to North Korea?

Zhao Zhenhui: But we can't do that yet (being sent back), because there is an American in the middle. If this American wants to sentence, it means that we are his witnesses.

Li Su: Are you talking about the pastor?

Zhao Zhenhui: Right. So they didn't want to send us to North Korea, they just kept us in a prison in Yanji.

Voice-over: The Yanbian Border Detachment Detention and Inspection Office is the only prison on the border between China and North Korea that temporarily holds illegal immigrants from North Korea. It is reported that it can accommodate hundreds of people. In August 2012, Zhao Zhenhui showed us such a painting by her sister at her home in the suburbs of Washington, USA.

Zhao Zhenhui: This is Room 4, we stayed for six months. This is room number 5, stayed for two months, and then changed back and forth. There are (rooms) here, in such a shape, there are also many rooms underneath. Just now a soldier came in and beat an old man and a pregnant woman, so we were very angry and started a hunger strike to quarrel with him. My sister remembered that day and began to paint.

This is glass on the outside, wire on the inside, and monitoring on the front. On one wall are all TVs (monitors), very small ones. There are two TVs (monitors) in one room, one in the bathroom, and one in the room. We all take a bath. When we started taking a shower, this man walking around (guards on duty) knew who had entered, and then told them from there. Many people ran into the monitoring (room). So the young people who just came in, they dare not wash. Sometimes we took a blanket to help them cover it, block it, and wash it. In this way, they said, are you going to dig a hole over there, or are you trying to hide money or something, and don't let us do that? Because of this, I went on a hunger strike with them for seven days.

After being detained for 15 months, Zhao Zhenhui and his sister were repatriated to North Korea.

*Ten thousand dollars to buy Shenglu*

Zhao Zhenhui: After we arrived in North Korea, we were already unidentified people. Because we did not participate in the president’s vote, we did not participate in it twice. After we arrived in North Korea, my sister and I lied in the name. North Korea does not have a computer, and the place where it was caught is different every time. What we say is what we mean. Stayed there for a month and then was released. After spending two months in North Korea, we found an opportunity to escape again.

Li Su: Escape to China again?

Zhao Zhenhui: Yes.

Li Su: Where did you go to China this time?

Zhao Zhenhui: First arrived is Yanji. Just past the river, two Korean clergymen were waiting for us. We took the car directly to Yanji. After a few days in Yanji, they found us a house. While living in that house, we suddenly discovered that those who collected 10,000 dollars in North Korea were arrested.

Li Su: That is to say, in fact, what you released was a channel for your church to use 10,000 dollars for dredge?

Zhao Zhenhui: Yes.

Li Su: Those who took the money were also found by North Korea and also arrested?

Zhao Zhenhui: Because when North Korea started to investigate this, we had been released and were no longer there. Then I knew someone had eaten the black money and let us out. I heard that six people had eaten the money. All of them were arrested and one was shot. The others didn't know where to go. The soldier who helped us cross the river was also arrested.

Zhao Zhenhui: Then I called the UN Refugee Agency, the phone number was given by the American pastor.

Li Su: Is it the office in Beijing?

Zhao Zhenhui: Right. The US government agreed to let us live there because it was done by UNHCR. At that time, the US government also participated in discussions with the Chinese government, and the Chinese government almost agreed. We lived there for about a year and three months. Both Americans and Koreans came out to interrogate us to see if we were North Koreans. After finding out that it was indeed the case, I agreed with China. China said: "This is Last Time."

Li Su: The last time.

Zhao Zhenhui: Yes, other North Koreans can no longer accept. They said so. Maybe the UNHCR people said: "OK", it was 2008.

Li Su: It was 2008, the year of the Olympics?

Zhao Zhenhui: Yes, before the (Olympic Games), we must send out all the (North Korean defectors). When I went to meet the people of the Public Security Bureau, they said (to me): "Don't talk about everything that happens in China." But I can't help but say.

*China: Some people protect me and some bad people sue me*

Li Su: Why do you think most of your countrymen, Koreans, escaped this country?

Zhao Zhenhui: In 1996, I was hungry, and I ran out only after trying to fill my stomach. After coming to China and seeing the life in China, they could not forget the life in China after being caught back, so they ran back again. (For) women, they have children here and have a family, so they are still here. The people after that are for freedom. In China, they know what freedom is, and they ran for freedom.

Li Su: Now the economic situation and living conditions of North Korea will be worse than before?

Zhao Zhenhui: I was arrested four times, almost every time I went back. The place has not changed at all. For almost ten years, nothing has changed. The market is almost the same. People who live a hard life are still so bitter. When I first went to China at the time, people in their 50s (in North Korea) are now dead, and people in their 30s are old. They are still alive. So many people are willing to come to China and let the Chinese help them. But it has been ten years and the Chinese are tired. Those fresh people on the border are no longer able to help them. The Chinese have stopped them from entering. If you knock on the door, I will sue you. So many people dare not come.

Li Su: When you were in China, you met a lot of people. Some people may be very bad for you, some people may have helped you. In general, what do you think of the Chinese?

Zhao Zhenhui: There are more bad guys, for me. But without those kindhearted people, I would not live to this day. Because there are good people protecting me, and bad people sue us, so we were arrested and suffered like that. But every time after we were released, we went to a house, entered the door, and knocked on the door, they all treated us well. Give us food, dress us, and buy medicine for us when we are sick. In China, it is not easy for people who grow land to save money. But they still gave us this money. Help us to go to Beijing and Shenyang by car. All the Chinese help on the way.

The Chinese are very good, I like them. But I don’t like the politics of the government because I have been arrested four times. Through this program, I want to let more Chinese understand the background of North Koreans. If the Chinese government listens to me, it will sympathize with the people of North Korea. Don't just send North Koreans back for the benefit of nations. Don't rush to grab them. Because many North Korean women were sold and gave birth to children. Their mother was arrested again in North Korea, and some have died in prison. Some dads became ill and became orphans. So I think there is no small problem between North Koreans and Chinese. I think the Chinese government can reconsider this issue and think for the better. Don't arrest them and send them back for the benefit of the country. If the North Korean government changes their minds and arrests us, they will not kill us, they will not be shot, or they will not be imprisoned. Under such circumstances, it does not matter if China is sent back. But we are here to live. I don’t know what politics is, but in order to escape these people who want to escape, and then send them back, this is tantamount to murder. Just like someone fell into the water and trampled him with their feet, so I think the Chinese government can treat them a little bit more? I just want to beg them.

Concluding remarks: The 2012 report of the United Nations Human Rights Council shows that there are still millions of North Koreans who are unable to eat, and the number of defectors is still rising. At the same time, China's repatriation of cross-border Koreans has never stopped. A correspondent in the North Korean online media "Daily NK" (DailyNK) in North Korea said: "Everyone here wants to escape, the only thing that keeps us away is the toughness of the Chinese authorities." I am Li Su and we will see you next time. .

          Congress report

          The United States will present the Snowden report, and the new film will make it a hero.

          2016.09.17 04:08
          • Voice of America

          Snowden gave a speech in Moscow via video. (September 24, 2015)

          The US House of Representatives Intelligence Committee issued a summary report detailing the non-confidential content of the committee's investigation into the US National Security Agency's former contractor Snowden's 2013 investigation of the theft of 1.5 million sensitive documents.

          According to the report of the Standing Special Intelligence Committee of the House of Representatives, the 36-page report is still classified as confidential for national security reasons.

          The Intelligence Commission’s two-year investigation yielded five main conclusions, first and foremost confirming that Snowden did cause “great harm” to national security. The report said that although Snowden insisted that he wanted to inform the Americans that the government was secretly monitoring them, most of the documents he stole involved US military, defense and intelligence projects.

          The Intelligence Committee said that second, Snowden is not a whistleblower. The so-called whistle-blower (the whistle-blowing person) means that a person's exposed confidential information exposes fraud, waste, abuse of power, bullying or other methods. But the committee said that the material leaked by Snowden did not reveal such illegal behavior.

          The report said that third, Snowden was criticized after the US National Security Agency executives had an argument with Snowden. Two weeks later, he began to download a large number of confidential documents.

          The House Intelligence Committee said that fourth, before he steals confidential documents, Snowden lied in a number of situations, or seriously exaggerated the facts. For example, Snowden claimed that he left the army because of a broken leg, but the fact is that he left because of a pain in his cheekbones. Snowden said that he has a diploma equivalent to high school graduation, but in fact he did not.

          Finally, the Intelligence Committee expressed concern that three years after the Snowden leak, the US National Security Agency, more broadly, did not take sufficient steps to ensure that similar leaks would not occur in the future.

          In the past few weeks, the Snowden incident has received more and more attention. At the same time, a film directed by Oliver Stone was released on Friday, which described Snowden as a hero and whistleblower.

          Human rights organizations and civil liberties rights organizations recently called on President Obama to pardon Snowden and allow him to return to the United States without having to face years of imprisonment. If Snowden is not forgiven, Snowden will be prosecuted for two charges in accordance with the anti-spyware law enacted in 1917.

          Critics say that Snowden handed over confidential documents to Russia and China, and the harm he caused was unavoidable. The Obama administration rejected a petition that was asked in the past to forgive Snowden.

          Snowden believes that if he returns to the United States, he may not be able to get a fair trial.

          In an exclusive interview with the New York Times, Snowden said: "The law even prohibits me from telling my jury about my motives. The question is - 'When you can't make a defense, can you have a fair trial?'"

          However, he thanked the people who had petitioned him. In the New York Times article he said: "Patriotic does not just mean that you agree with your government."

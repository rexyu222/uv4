        North Korea has sent goods and related missile technology to Syria for its ballistic missile and chemical weapons programs, in violation of U.N. sanctions, U.N. experts say. North Korea has also sent a banned ballistic missile system to Myanmar.

        A panel of experts overseeing sanctions against North Korea said an investigation into Pyongyang's transfer of embargoed ballistic missiles, conventional weapons and dual-use goods found that between 2012 and 2017, more than 40 previously unreported shipments were sent to Syria.

        The panel said an unnamed U.N. member state also reported that Myanmar had received evidence of various conventional weapons from North Korea, including multiple rocket launchers and surface-to-air missiles in addition to ballistic missile systems.

        The Associated Press reported on February 2nd that North Korea had defied U.N. sanctions on oil and gas and cooperated with ballistic missiles banned by Syria and Myanmar, bringing in nearly $200 million in illegal exports in the last nine months alone.

        The Associated Press obtained more than 200 pages of reports Tuesday night, including the panel's findings on Syria's chemical weapons. The United States and other Western countries have accused Syria of using chemical weapons in rebel-held areas, including the recent use of chemical weapons in areas near Damascus, a claim that Assad's government has always denied.

        Ballistic missiles and chemical weapons.

        The report, to the UN Security Council, which is expected to be released in mid-March, details "a wealth of new evidence" of North Korea's dealings with Syria since 2008.

        A North Korean technical delegation "participated in the transfer of special resistance valves and thermometers known to be used for chemical weapons to Syria" in 2016, the report said.

        The report said the information came from another U.N. member state, which also reported that North Korean technicians "continue to work at chemical weapons and missile facilities in Baze, Adra and Hama."

        "Syria does not have North Korean technology companies, only some North Koreans who are private contractors, who are only allowed to operate in stadiums and train athletes," the report quoted Syria as saying in response to these sources. "

        The experts added that they had not yet received a response to the relevant documents requesting support for the claim, nor did they have a list of all North Koreans who had travelled to Syria.

        China consignment.

        The panel said it also inspected shipments sent to companies in Damascus in 2016 and 2017 by Cheng Tong Trading Co. Ltd. The goods were seized by United Nations Member States.

        Experts say the 13 containers are filled with "acid-resistant tiles" that will cover 5,000 square meters, enough for large-scale industrial projects.

        Some analysts concluded that the tiles "will be used for activities at high temperatures", while others say the material "can be used to brick the interior walls of chemical plants." "

    Uncover the core institutions of Kim III's iron-fisted administration.

    • Comments.
    • Share.

    Washington - Washington

    In the eyes of the outside world, the North Korean regime for more than 20 years of diplomatic difficulties, has been in the wind and rain. However, kim's regime has moved to a third generation, and Kim Jong-un, in his twenties, appears to have gradually seized power. Experts say the key to Kim's regime's failure is to rely on a special body with great power.

    North Korea's special body is the powerful Ministry of Organizational Guidance, the core authority of the Pyongyang regime, whose job is to maintain the stability of the Kim regime.

    Reporting by Robert Collins, Author of "The Republic of Pyongyang" (AP)
    x
    《平壤共和国》作者罗伯特·柯林斯发表谈话（美国之音拍摄）

    It oversees and monitors all officials, as well as the Central Organization Department, the Ministry of Supervision, the Ministry of Security and other functions, some say it is North Korea's "Gestapo." Robert Collins, an American expert on North Korea, says the agency controls almost everything in the party.

    "There is a control body within the Korean Workers' Party, called the Ministry of Organizational Guidance, composed of 1,300 people, most of whom are in Pyongyang, some of them in various provinces and counties, to ensure that the party performs its duties," he said. "

    The party's duty is to be loyal to the supreme leader.

    Robert Collins, a former chief strategic adviser to the Joint U.S.-South Korean Military Command in Seoul, said in a February report entitled "The Republic of Pyongyang" that the agency plays a particularly important role, and that it is arguably the Politburo, the Central Military Commission, the Monitoring Committee and the Central Committee.

    "It's the agency that examines the loyalty of every North Korean official to the government, and every week reports to the supreme leader, and if you don't do well, if you don't meet the requirements, you're going to be cleared," Collins said. "

    Anyone who knows a little about North Korea knows what it means to be purged. Zhang Sung-taek, the former second-in-command of the North Korean regime and kim Jong-un's aunt, was executed in December 2013 on charges of conspiring to overthrow the government.

    Collins said the arrest and execution of Kim Jong-un's aunt, Zhang Cheng-ze, was ordered by Cho Yong-jun, the head of the organization's guidance department.

    Soldiers march during a military parade to mark Kim Il Sung's birthday in North Korea (April 15, 2012)
    x
    在朝鲜纪念金日成生日的庆典阅兵中，军人在行进（2012年4月15日）

    It was also reported that Kim Jong-un executed Defense Minister Hyun Yong-chul on April 30 last year on charges that appeared to be dozing off during a military operation. Yonhap said Kim Jong-un believes Hyun Yong-chul's dozing shows disrespect for his disloyalty.

    Lee Yong-jee, chief of staff of the Korean People's Army, was executed in early February on charges of "sectarian and authoritarian corruption," Yonhap reported.

    Collins's report says that both Justice Department officials, foreign ministry officials and even all senior officials of the Central Committee are subject to the organizational guidance department's examination to ensure their loyalty to the Kim dynasty.

    The Ministry of Organizational Guidance will report the results of the examination to the supreme leader, who, on the advice of the Ministry of Organizational Guidance, punishes the institutions and officials who maintain the weak regime.

    The more support and loyalty the supreme leader, Kim Jong-un, and his rule, the more benefits they get, including housing, promotion and material benefits.

    This department is not made up of any professional, military, scientific, educational or economic experts, and its great power comes from simply following the instructions of North Korea's supreme leader, and no individual or agency can assign a mandate to the department.

    In 1974, When Kim Jong Il was head of the organization's guidance department, he set up a monitoring group within the department to ensure that the heads of each field were loyal to the supreme leader.

    In North Korea, each person belongs to at least one party organization, either the Workers' Party or the Party's affiliated organizations, such as the Women's Federation, the Kim Il Sung Socialist Youth League, or other party-led groups. Each person has a file that is assessed according to the life of the participating party organizationand and the implementation of the policies of the authorities.

    The Ministry of Organizational Guidance is also responsible for the recruitment, training and appointment of all Party members and cadres.

    Collins says the system and its rigour, the cost of the counter-rule sand, and the rewards are so superior that it's hard to change. He said that if the regime is expected to change, unless there is a change in the ministry of organizational guidance.

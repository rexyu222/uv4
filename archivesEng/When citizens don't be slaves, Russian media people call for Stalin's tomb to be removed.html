Moscow -

On the 66th anniversary of Stalin's death, influential Russian media outlets have called for Stalin's tomb to be removed from Moscow's Red Square. Many also called for legislation to prohibit the glorification and deification of the former Soviet dictator. But the Russian Communists still went to Stalin's tomb to lay flowers and mourn.

Stalin was a national disgrace.

A prominent Russian journalist, Sergey Darenko, called on Tuesday that Stalin was a disgrace to the Russian state and the nation and that Russian society should have the courage to remove dirt and remove Stalin's tomb from Moscow's Red Square immediately.

Although many see Stalin as a dictator and bloody tyrant, in today's Putin-ruled Russia, Stalin has become increasingly admired and welcomed in recent years. March 5 marks the 66th anniversary of Stalin's death. Dalenko said that when he hosted the radio station's connecting program that day, he found that a large portion of the audience was thinking about the day with a kneeling attitude toward Stalin, and he felt he had to appeal.

Darenko is currently the editor-in-chief of Radio Moscow speaks. He has been active in Russian media since the collapse of the Soviet Union. In particular, in the late 1990s and early 2000s, he was one of the leaders of the largest official first television show and a political channel host. At the time, A coalition of Putin's political rivals, former Moscow mayor Luzhkov and former Prime Minister Dmitry Primakov, was also widely supported, and Mr. Dalenko was seen as playing a key role in upholding Mr. Putin and boosting his popularity. But soon after he came to power, he parted ways with him.

Childish and slavery trends intensified Stalin was a symbol

Darenko said that the "Stalin's comeback" shows that today's Russian society is increasingly childish and slavery. He says the fondness for Stalin shows that many people volunteer to be slaves and let dictators slaughter as if they were livestock, and that these people don't want to build civil society and be a part of it.

'Many people in Russian society are now calling for an iron fist to rule, willing to be a slave and not want to be a citizen, ' Mr. Darenko said. 'The trend is intensifying, and Stalin is a symbol of such a slave society.'

It is impossible to liquidate Stalin under Putin

Historian Zubov said that as long as Putin remained in power, Russia would not be able to liquidate Stalin or remove Stalin's tomb from the Red Square.

Zubov said that while Putin also participated in the unveiling of the monument to political persecution, Putin was an admirer of Stalin and was more deeply attached to the Soviet Union.

The ruling power and Stalin's secret police

Zubov said that the vast majority of the current ruling power comes from the Soviet security apparatus, which was formerly Stalin's secret police, the secret police is Stalin's favorite, the background that led him to believe that the reerected statue of Stalin in recent years will not be pushed.

People are naive and don't understand Stalin's crimes.

Zubov, from the family of senior Soviet naval generals, said the corruption and social injustices that pervad edited Many in Russia today naively nostalgic for Stalin. But his family experience is clear, and in the stalin era, when the so-called iron-fisted rule was over, social injustice and corruption were rampant.

Zubov said that some of the historical archives that were made public after the collapse of the Soviet Union were kept secret again, that the media now lacked freedom, and that there were few accounts of Stalin's political persecution in primary and secondary school textbooks, leading to a growing lack of understanding and ignorance of Stalin's crimes. He said it was hard to imagine Hitler being worshipped again in Germany.

Call for legislation to ban the glorification of dictators

Descendants of a group of victims of Stalin's purge also called tuesday for Russia to legislate against deification and glorification of Stalin. Niesgerenko's father, an 82-year-old retiree, was executed during Stalin's purge in 1937. Stalin's signature was on the death penalty.

Niesgerenko said similar laws were necessary to legislate against denial of political persecution and to prevent Communists from organizing people to lay flowers in Stalin's cemetery to mourn. To that end, he and other activists have collected more than 10,000 endorsements on the Internet for more than two years.

You can't cover up the crime forever.

So far, many of the crimes committed by Stalin and the Soviet regime have remained firmly hidden, Niesgerenko said.

Niesgerenko: "I remember when we were all singing and passionateaboutaboutabouts in building the so-called new world. But massacres and political persecution are also taking place in parallel, and many have disappeared as a result. Such crimes should never be covered up forever. "

Russian Communist Sanduer continues to worship Stalin

Hundreds of Russian Communists laid flowers at Stalin's tomb in Moscow's Red Square on Tuesday. Many members of the Russian Communist Party said that Stalin led the Soviet Union to industrialization and led the Soviet Union to victory in World War II.

After laying flowers, Russian Communist Party leader and State Duma lawmaker Kashin said that this year is the 140th anniversary of Stalin's birth, the more time goes back, the more you can feel Stalin as the Soviet leader's greatness.

In recent years, supporters of the Russian Republic and Stalin have held annual commemorations on March 5th, the day of Stalin's death, and on December 18th, Stalin's birth. The anniversary of Stalin's birth will be even bigger at the end of the year.

Demonstrations at the memorial service Stalin should go to hell

At a russian communist party's wreath-laying event at Stalin's tomb on Tuesday, two activists from Russia's "de-communist" movement threw broken flowers at Stalin's tomb, chanting slogans such as Stalin being the executioner and Stalin going to hell. They were then taken away by Russian supporters and police officers present.

Many on local social media groups praised the activists' actions as an example for others.

Celebrating Stalin's death, killing more alive,

The day of Stalin's death was also regarded by many as a holiday. Ali Bartz, a well-known journalist, said she often celebrates her day with a bottle of vodka with friends. She said Lenin's tomb in Red Square should also be removed along with Stalin's tomb.

Some historians say the longer Stalin is alive, the more people will be killed. If Stalin lived for a few more days, the Soviet Jews would be subjected to mass exile and distribution, Zubov said.

Stalin's crimes are over after his death.

Historian Hlevnyuk said that after Stalin's death, the Soviet regime immediately adjusted its policies. The Gulag concentration camp was disbanded and the Korean War ended.

Edman, a sociologist, said Stalin's crimes were a long list, and that among those persecuted were Stalin supporters and relatives of the tyrant, in addition to his political opponents and dislikes.

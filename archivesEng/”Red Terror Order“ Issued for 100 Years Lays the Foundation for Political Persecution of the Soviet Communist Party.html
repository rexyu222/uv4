          The Bolshevik government, which came to power after the October revolution, formally ordered the start of "red terror" 100 years ago. "Red Terror" as a major state policy not only let the Bolshevik regime open fire, but also reflected the brutal characteristics of the new regime under Lenin, and later Stalin's purge and soviet political persecution laid the foundation.

          Issued "Red Terror" Order

          100 years ago, on September 5, 1918, the Bolshevik government issued an order for the beginning of "red terror". The order says that maintaining rear order through terrorist means is essential.

          The order said that in order to ensure the security of the Soviet regime, class enemies must be isolated and sent to concentration camps. At the same time, all persons associated with the White Army and the rebellion, as well as the plot, should be executed.

          Killing ring open

          In September of that year, the Bolshevik government executed more than 500 ministers, military officers and university professors from the former Tsarist government in St. Petersburg. Later, workers in Yekaterinburg in the Urals held a mass rally against the Bolshevik government, which was suppressed by fire fired by the Bolshevik secret police, Cheika, and dozens of workers were killed.

          At that time, the Russian civil war had begun, and the Bolshevik regime had detained a large number of White Army personnel. After the "Red Terror" order was issued, many hostages were executed.

          "Red terror" is the national policy to destroy the entire social class.

          In addition to targeting former government officials, military officers, entrepreneurs, businessmen, Orthodox monks and workers, farmers, especially rich farmers, who refused to cooperate in the Bolshevik Red Army's compulsory food collection, were also executed in large numbers.

          Historian Sokolov said that "red terror" was one of the main policies of the Bolshevik regime at the time. "Red Terror" not only targets political opponents, but also a large number of innocent people. "Red Terror" is aimed not only at the individual, but also at the entire social class. He believes that more than two million people could be killed as a result of the "red terror".

          In 1919, the Bolshevik government again ordered the establishment of large numbers of concentration camps throughout the country. The earliest concentration camps were built in the Arctic Circle.

          The Great Famine, the Great Cleansing, the Revolution

          The form of "Red Terror" lasted until 1922. When the Russian Civil War ended, Lenin began to pursue the "New Economic Policy". But the terrorist means of governance of the Bolsheviks continue in other ways. This was followed by the Great Famine of the 1930s, followed by the Stalin purge.

          Many historians believe that the "red terror" laid the foundation for the subsequent Stalin revolution and the political persecution of the Soviet regime.

          The bloody horrors later took the German invasion as liberation.

          Historian Petrov says that "red terror", political persecution, looting of peasants and brutal killings all had an important impact on later history. For example, after the outbreak of World War II, the Soviet Red Army was defeated in the early days of the war, and millions of Red Army officers and soldiers surrendered to the Nazis without resistance, all of which were related to the "red terror".

          Petrov: "I am convinced that there would have been no such thing as the Soviets who had worked with Nazi Germany to serve the Germans without the bloody horrors and persecutions that would have been so traumatic for the people and the bloodthat they could not forget." It was these horrors and persecutions that led to the anger of a large part of the population that led to the liberation of the German attack and occupation during the 1942 war in 1942. "

          Lenin's assassination provides excuse for "red terror"

          A few days before the Bolshevik regime issued the "Red Terror" order, on 30 August 1918, the assassinations of Lenin and Petrogradchka leader Uritzki took place in Moscow and St. Petersburg, respectively. Lenin was seriously wounded and Ulitsky was killed.

          Kaplan, the suspectined killer of Lenin, was executed without trial under the Kremlin wall a few days later. Kaplan's body was then burned in oil drums by Bolshevik militants.

          Lenin's assassination is still considered a pending case. Many have so far questioned Kaplan's ability to shoot Lenin. Some historians even believe that Lenin's assassination may have involved Bolshevik infighting. But these two incidents provided an excuse for the Bolshevik regime to order the start of "red terror".

          "Red Terror" started earlier, secret police are tools of implementation.

          Historians believe that the "red terror" actually began earlier. Even after the October Revolution, the Bolshevik regime began to persecute. In July 1918, the last Tsar and his family, as well as the servants, were executed by the Bolsheviks in Yekaterinburg.

          In December 1917, the Bolsheviks formed the Secret Police Organization, the People's Anti-Government Committee, known as Cheika. Cheika is an important tool for implementing "Red Terror". The Cheka militants had enormous powers to execute so-called counter-revolutionaries, breakers and other suspects on the roadside without a court.

          Cheika is also considered a precursor to the Soviet secret police, the KGB and today's Russian security services. Late last year, President Putin and many senior Kremlin officials attended the celebration of the 100th anniversary of the founding of Russia's security intelligence agency.

          Lenin actively promoted the blood on his hands like Stalin.

          The then Chika leader, Terzinski, who was involved in the execution of the last Tsar's Bolshevik leaders in the Urals, Sverdlov and Lenin, were active advocates and advocates of "red terror". Historians believe that Lenin and the Bolsheviks included terror and violence in their organizational programmes of action more than a decade before the October revolution.

          After the October Revolution, the Bolsheviks began to destroy and loot Orthodox churches and property on a large scale. Resistance against the clergy. Lenin had ordered that the more people were killed, the better. In terms of terror and persecution, Lenin had no less blood on his hands than Stalin, historian Zuboff said.

          "Red Terror" With Russian Civil War

          After the Tsar stepped down from the Post-February Revolution in 1917, the political situation in Russia, which was taking part in a major war, was thrown into chaos when the pro-Western interim government in Russia was left in disarray over its incompetence. The Well-organized, but not the Bolsheviks, the main political force in Russian politics, took the opportunity to stage a coup that seized power with little resistance.

          Elections for Russia's Constituent Assembly were held almost immediately after the October Revolution, but the Bolsheviks suffered a crushing defeat. The Bolsheviks dissolved the Constituent Assembly by force in early 1918, and the Russian civil war broke out.

          "Red Terror" Distinguishes "White Terror"

          Many supporters of the Russian Communist Party believe that it was the "red terror" that preserved the new regime. "Red Terror" is a response to the "white terror" carried out by the White Army.

          Many historians believe that "white terror" and "red terror" cannot be compared. "White Terror" is carried out by White Army field commanders and generals in the front-line areas and is much smaller than "Red Terror". Moreover, "white terror" is not as good as "red terror", which was a major official policy pursued by the Government that year.

          A hundred years later, Russia and Ukraine reacted differently.

          On the centenary of the "Red Terror", russian officials have no activity and there is little media coverage. A special seminar was organized by the "Memorial" human rights organization in Moscow.

          Ukrainian officials a few days ago suggested that major television stations should stop entertainment on 5 September in memory of the victims of the "red terror" of a century ago.

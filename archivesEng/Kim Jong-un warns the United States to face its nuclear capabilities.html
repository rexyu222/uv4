          Comments (19)

          North Korean leader Kim Jong-un said that the United States must face up to the facts of the North Korean nuclear program.

          Kim Jong-un warned in his annual New Year speech Monday that he had a nuclear button on his desk. "The entire United States is in the scope of our nuclear strikes, and the United States can never launch a war against me and our country," he said.

          Kim Jong-un said that North Korea can deal with any nuclear threat from the United States and has a strong nuclear deterrent to prevent the United States from playing with fire.

          He said: "The United States must realize that this is not extortion, but facts."

          Kim Jong-un’s New Year’s speech is widely regarded as a signal he sends in policy directions and priorities in the coming year.

          Last year, Kim Jong-un and US President Trump’s war of words continued to escalate. Trump warned North Korea that if it threatened the United States, it would face "cannon and anger."

          Pyongyang said it would consider testing intercontinental ballistic missiles in the waters off Guam, the US Pacific. After that, North Korea fired two long-range missiles in the direction of Japan and conducted the sixth nuclear test in September.

          The war of words between the two sides continued until September when Trump gave a speech at the UN General Assembly, when he compared Kim Jong-un to the "rocket man" who performed suicide missions.

          Kim Jong-un called Trump "an old confused" and accused him of being "insanity."

          In November last year, North Korea announced that it had achieved the goal of developing intercontinental ballistic missile capabilities after launching a "Mars-15" intercontinental ballistic missile with a range that could reach the United States.
